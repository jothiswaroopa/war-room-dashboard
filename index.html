the golden rule of systems
01If you do it more than twice, write it down as a process. If you write it down, automate it. If you automate it, delegate it.
02A system doesn't need to be perfect. A rough SOP followed consistently beats a perfect one nobody uses. Done beats perfect.
03Before handing any task to a hire, record yourself doing it on Loom once. That video is their training. That video is the SOP.
04Every time something goes wrong, update the SOP so it can't happen again. Systems improve through problems, not despite them.
05Your goal: any task in your agency can be handed to someone new in under 30 minutes of reading/watching the SOP. If it can't, the SOP is incomplete.

At Day 90 (September 7, 2026), pause for one hour and answer these 4 questions. Not what you planned — what actually happened. If you score 3+, continue the plan as-is. If you score 2 or below, the plan needs a route correction — which is not failure, it's data.
